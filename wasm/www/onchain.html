How it works

Your browser sends keypresses. The chain does everything else.

Input

Keypresses become extrinsics

Every keypress is collected and submitted as a signed Substrate extrinsic via doom.submitInput. Your browser has zero game logic — it only knows how to send transactions and paint pixels.

Execution

Validators tick the game

Every 500ms, the chain produces a block. The on_initialize hook reads queued inputs, runs physics, AI, collision detection — the full DOOM engine — and writes the new game state to storage.

Rendering

Frames rendered by the chain

Your browser calls doom_renderFrame RPC. The validator raycasts the scene from on-chain state, composites sprites, and returns 320×200 raw RGBA pixels. Your device just paints them to a canvas.
